Central Bank Policy Implications

The Federal Reserve: The Fed remains anchored at an upper bound of 3.75%, contrary to early expectations of sharper 4.00%+ hikes. A broad-based dollar weakness has taken hold as market participants digest a more dovish reality than previously priced in.

The European Central Bank: Despite structural growth headwinds in the Eurozone, the ECB has successfully maintained its deposit rate at 2.00% since February. This steadfastness has fueled a remarkable rally in EUR/USD, reflecting a pure USD weakness narrative rather than an overwhelming Eurozone economic renaissance.

The Bank of Japan: The BOJ held its rate at 0.75%, but the underlying dynamics have shifted. With 3 board members voting to hike, the hawkish undercurrent is undeniable. However, structural carry trade dynamics have proven incredibly resilient, keeping the Yen surprisingly weak.

FX Strategy Outlook

Our core thesis centers on the structural retreat of the US Dollar across major pairs. The EUR/USD trajectory toward 1.1791 defies traditional growth differentials, underscoring that global liquidity dynamics and Fed repricing are in the driver's seat. For the Yen, despite the hawkish dissent at the BOJ, the carry trade remains highly attractive, resulting in a counter-intuitive USD/JPY sustained near the 158–159 handle. GBP/USD has also benefited tremendously from the dollar sell-off, surging to 1.3544. Strategically, we favor long EUR exposures while remaining highly tactical on JPY interventions.
